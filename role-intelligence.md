# Role Intelligence

## Real mandate
Lead the operating layer that converts practice demand into a concentrated, governed, adopted, and measurable portfolio of AI-enabled legal workflows, while developing practice-aligned managers and turning proven learning into reusable firm capability.

## Central tensions
- Practice judgment vs. firm-wide reuse
- Speed and attorney leverage vs. privilege and defensibility
- Model capability vs. knowledge readiness
- Central standards vs. partnership and practice autonomy
- Pilot volume vs. portfolio concentration
- AI assistance vs. retained human legal authority
- Build flexibility vs. vendor, integration, security, and lifecycle burden

## Candidate thesis
Build the common law of AI-enabled work.

## Operating model
Applied AI Reporter: Fact Pattern -> Knowledge Record -> Human Authority -> Evaluation -> Adoption / Ownership -> Value Evidence -> Precedent Treatment.

## Success measures
Use contextual categories, not invented targets: flow, quality, knowledge, authority/risk, adoption, value, reuse, lifecycle.

## Strongest objection
No direct law-firm or legal-technology tenure. The candidate-facing response leads with verified strength in applied AI operating design, high-reliability quality, team leadership, change, executive-technical translation, and outcome ownership, while treating legal-domain fluency as a disciplined day-one immersion requirement.
